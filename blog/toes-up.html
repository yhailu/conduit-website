Toes Up: The Rise of Philly's Underground Poker Legend

📅 Mar 25, 2026 ⏱️ 6 min read

In the smoky back rooms of South Philadelphia, where the cheesesteaks are greasy and the bets are greasier, there's a name that's been whispered at every felt table for the last decade: "Toes Up" Tommy DiNardo.

Nobody knows exactly how he got the nickname. Some say it's because every opponent who sits across from him ends up "toes up" — flat broke, staring at the ceiling, wondering what just happened. Others claim it's because Tommy kicks his shoes off under the table for good luck. He's never confirmed either story. "A magician never reveals his secrets," he says with a grin, shuffling a chip across his knuckles without looking.

From Kensington to the Final Table

Tommy grew up in Kensington, the kind of neighborhood where you learned to read people before you learned to read books. By 14, he was running penny poker games in his grandmother's basement. By 18, he'd been banned from three casinos in Atlantic City — not for cheating, but for winning too consistently.

"They said I was 'disrupting the ecosystem,'" Tommy recalls, laughing. "I didn't even know what that meant. I just knew I was good at cards."

"Poker isn't about the cards you're holding. It's about the story you're telling. And in Philly, we tell the best stories."

The $50 Buy-In That Changed Everything

The legend really began in 2019 at a no-name tournament in a VFW hall off Passyunk Ave. Tommy walked in with $50, a hoagie in his jacket pocket, and what he describes as "a feeling." Eight hours later, he walked out with $47,000 and a reputation that would follow him across the East Coast poker circuit.

What made that night legendary wasn't just the money — it was how he played. In the final hand, holding nothing but a pair of threes, Tommy stared down a Philadelphia Eagles lineman (name withheld for legal reasons) who was sitting on a full house. Tommy went all in. The lineman folded.

"I could see it in his eyes," Tommy said later. "He had a great hand. But he didn't have the stomach for it. In Philly, stomach is everything."

The Philosophy of Toes Up

Ask Tommy about his strategy and he'll tell you it has nothing to do with math. "The nerds with their GTO charts and solver outputs — they're playing a different game," he says. "I'm playing people. I'm playing fear. I'm playing ego. I'm playing that third beer they had an hour ago."

His rules are simple:

🃏 Tommy's Rules of the Table

1. Never play scared money. If you can't afford to lose it, you've already lost.
2. Talk. A lot. Most players are terrified of silence. Use that.
3. Order food at the table. Nothing rattles an opponent like watching you eat a cheesesteak while going all-in.
4. Wear comfortable shoes. Or better yet, take them off. Toes up.

Where Is He Now?

These days, Tommy splits his time between a weekly home game in Fishtown, occasional trips to Borgata, and mentoring younger players who remind him of himself — hungry, broke, and absolutely convinced they're the best player in the room.

"Every kid in Philly thinks they can play poker," Tommy says. "Most of them are right. The difference between good and great is whether you can take the hit when the river doesn't go your way and still come back the next night like nothing happened."

He pauses, takes a sip of his coffee, and adds: "That's not just poker. That's Philly."

"You want to know why they call me Toes Up? Because when I sit down at a table, I'm the most relaxed person in the room. And relaxed people win. Tense people donate."
